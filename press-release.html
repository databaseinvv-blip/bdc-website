Borneo Development Capital announces formation of Sarawak-focused private equity fund.

Kuala Lumpur-based BDC PLT incorporated to deploy long-horizon capital into Sarawak's infrastructure, public-market equity, and digital economy through the 2030 development cycle.

Borneo Development Capital / Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia / For media enquiries: borneodc.invest@gmail.com

KUALA LUMPUR — Borneo Development Capital (BDC), a newly established Malaysian private equity firm, has announced its formation as a place-based investment vehicle focused exclusively on the long-term development of Sarawak.

The firm has been incorporated in Malaysia as Borneo Development Capital PLT (Registration: 202504003274, LLP0044794-LGN) and is structured to deploy capital across three complementary strategies: government and infrastructure financing, structured participation in listed Malaysian equity, and selective high-growth investments in digital infrastructure and future-economy sectors.

BDC's mandate is anchored in the Sarawak Development Strategy 2030 (SDS 2030), the state's published economic blueprint, which targets a doubling of state GDP to RM282 billion by the end of the decade. The firm is positioned to participate in the infrastructure, energy transition, and digital economy investments expected to underpin this trajectory.

Investment thesis

BDC's central thesis is structural: Sarawak's combination of abundant renewable energy, stable climate, vast underused land, and an articulated policy framework creates one of the most distinctive long-horizon investment opportunities in Southeast Asia. The firm has summarised this view in its founding thesis, titled "From Watt to Byte," which describes how the state's clean energy advantage underwrites the digital infrastructure expansion expected through the back half of the decade.

The firm intends to construct a portfolio that pairs the defensive cashflow characteristics of infrastructure and government-linked financing with the upside exposure of structured public-market equity and selective digital growth platforms. The objective is capital preservation alongside meaningful long-horizon returns aligned with the state's development trajectory.

Senior advisory bench

BDC is supported by a senior advisory bench drawn from institutional capital markets across Hong Kong and Asia. The advisory board includes Derek Cheng, Senior Advisor at the Blackstone Group and former Chief Executive Officer and Managing Director of China Resources Capital, and Lewis Wan, Chairman of the Hong Kong Financial Asset Exchange Group, who serves as a HKSI Senior Fellow and SFC Responsible Officer.

The firm's General Partners combine licensed financial advisory experience, Hong Kong-regulated asset management, M&A advisory across hundreds of transactions, and direct operating experience in Sarawak's construction, civil engineering, and infrastructure sectors over the past two decades.

Investment focus

BDC's investment focus mirrors the development priorities of the Sarawak Development Strategy 2030 framework. The firm will deploy capital across four sectoral themes:

  • Infrastructure — roads, bridges, water systems, and the built environment supporting Sarawak's industrial and demographic expansion.
  • Renewable Energy — hydropower, solar, and energy storage projects anchoring the state's structural cost advantage.
  • Digital Infrastructure — data-centre clusters, smart city systems, and connectivity infrastructure positioning Sarawak as a regional digital hub.
  • Listed Equity — selective participation in public-market opportunities including IPO, pre-IPO, and structured situations on Bursa Malaysia.

About Borneo Development Capital

Borneo Development Capital (BDC) is a Sarawak-focused private equity firm based in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. The firm deploys long-horizon capital across infrastructure, public-market equity, and digital infrastructure aligned with the Sarawak Development Strategy 2030. BDC is structured to combine institutional capital markets experience with on-the-ground operating knowledge of Sarawak, and operates under the Securities Commission Malaysia regulatory framework.
